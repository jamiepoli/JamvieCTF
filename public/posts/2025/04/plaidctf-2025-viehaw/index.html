Skyrim’s Restoration Loop

Okay so, if you ever played Skyrim, you may be aware of this infamous restoration loop hack. For whatever reason, there exists a glitch where (under special circumstances) equipping and then un-equipping some set of armor that is supposed to grant you a stat buff while equipped will still hold a reference to the buff even if you remove the armor. Therefore, re-equipping that piece of armor will re-add the buff, giving you twice the benefits, or 3x or 4x or etc etc…

Clever players found this bug and ended up utilizing it to buff their stats to high-heaven on console versions of Skyrim (and PC as well if you wanted to get god mode the way Todd unintended), letting them do cool things like punch a dragon to death or suplex the ebony warrior. The details are listed in this YouTube video if you’re interested in learning more about it.

The main principle of this glitch is basically this: the program forgets to properly remove a de-referenced buff, then proceeds to add the same buff again onto the player’s “effects stack”, for lack of a better word. Players repeat the action to de-reference the buff in one check (armor is removed - check) but not the other (buff is removed - nope), allowing them to re-add it again. I tried to emulate the principle here, but of course because I coded this in TypeScript and I am bad at mastering the art of it, there existed many ECMAScript Fun And Cute Quirks that created a whole bunch of unintended solutions. Honestly, I found them all to be very unique and fun, so I’m not too mad about it. I still have a whole bunch of other video-game inspired glitches that I can use for future challenges.

Another goof I did was instantiate a single instance of me, instead of on-demand at the duel endpoint. This basically made me a raid boss, so people could drain my health continually without worry and I genuinely found that to be extremely fuckin hilarious so I’m noting that here.
